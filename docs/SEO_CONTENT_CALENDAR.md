---

## Strategy Overview

This calendar covers 12 weeks of blog content for bestbottles.com at a cadence of two posts per week. That is the right answer for where Best Bottles is right now.

Once a week is not enough to build search momentum in a competitive category. Three times a week is too fast to maintain quality at launch, and quality is the only thing that earns Google's trust in 2025. Two posts per week gives the content team time to write something worth reading, gives the editorial calendar breathing room, and compounds faster than a weekly drip.

Every post in this calendar is written to rank, to be read, and to drive a specific commercial action. These are not content for content's sake. They are assets.

---

## The Copywriting Framework

Each post draws from one of three copywriting traditions depending on its objective:

**Gary Halbert** — Direct response structure. Problem identification, emotional stakes, specific solution, clear call to action. Used for decision-stage posts where the reader is close to a purchase.

**David Ogilvy** — Authority and narrative. Long-form credibility building. Specific facts, confident tone, never boastful. Used for brand story posts and technical authority pieces.

**Eugene Schwartz** — Desire amplification. The reader already wants what we sell. The copy channels and focuses that desire. Used for aspirational posts targeting scaling brands.

---

## Content Pillars

**Educational Expertise (40%)** — 10 posts. How-to guides, material comparisons, industry knowledge, technical specifications. Ranks for informational searches. Builds trust.

**Product Guidance / Spotlight (35%)** — 8 posts. Specific SKU families, use cases, comparisons, decision guides. Ranks for product searches. Drives catalog traffic.

**Brand Stories (25%)** — 6 posts. Heritage, retail proof, scaling stories, the Nemat supply chain. Ranks for brand and supplier searches. Converts consideration-stage visitors.

---

## SEO Principles

- One primary keyword cluster per post, chosen for intent and volume, not vanity
- Internal linking to product catalog on every post, minimum two product families
- Specific over vague: the more specific the claim, the more it is believed
- No hedge phrases, no filler, no content that reads like it was produced for its own sake
- Every title should make the reader feel they would be missing something important if they did not read it

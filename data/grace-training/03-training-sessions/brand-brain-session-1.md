## **1\. Company Identity & Differentiation**

### **Who is Best Bottles?**

Best Bottles is the B2B division of Nemat International, Inc., a fragrance company with over two decades of industry expertise. Unlike commodity suppliers (SKS, Berlin Packaging), Best Bottles operates as a **"boutique" specialty supplier** focused exclusively on:

* Perfumes and fragrance oils  
* Essential oils and aromatherapy  
* Eau de Parfums (EDPs)  
* Skincare and serums

### **Key Differentiators**

1. **Practitioner Expertise:** Best Bottles uses the same bottles they sell for Nemat products sold at Ulta, Sephora, and Whole Foods. If it's good enough for retail, it's validated for professional use.  
2. **System Guarantee:** Components sold together (bottles \+ caps \+ applicators) are guaranteed to fit. No mixing-and-matching from different manufacturers.  
3. **Small Quantity Sets:** Customers can buy as few as 5 pieces that work together perfectly.  
4. **Proprietary Innovation:** Custom-engineered roll-on systems with precision ball sizing (9.98mm to 10.04mm tolerance testing).

## **3\. Product Recommendation Decision Tree**

When helping customers, Grace should follow this sequence:

### **Step 1: Identify Product Type**

Ask: "What will you be filling these bottles with?"

* Perfume oil (thick, oil-based)  
* EDP/cologne (thin, alcohol-based)  
* Essential oil (varies)  
* Lotion/cream (thick)  
* Serum (medium)  
* Beard/hair oil (medium to thick)

### **Step 2: Determine Viscosity**

* **Thin liquids:** Alcohol-based EDPs, water-based products  
* **Medium viscosity:** Serums, facial oils, lighter oils  
* **Thick liquids:** Lotions, creams, thick perfume oils

### **Step 3: Recommend Applicator Type**

| Viscosity | Recommended Applicator |
| ----- | ----- |
| Thin (alcohol-based) | Spray, atomizer, fine mist |
| Medium (oils, serums) | Dropper, roll-on, orifice reducer |
| Thick (lotions, creams) | Pump, wide-mouth jar |

**⚠️ CRITICAL:** Roll-on bottles are designed for OIL viscosity. Thin alcohol-based liquids WILL LEAK through roll-on applicators.

### **Step 4: Determine Capacity**

| Use Case | Typical Capacity |
| ----- | ----- |
| Samples/testers | 1-5ml |
| Travel size | 10-15ml |
| Standard retail | 30-50ml |
| Large/value size | 60-120ml |
| Bulk/refill | 120ml+ |

### **Step 5: Consider Aesthetics**

Ask: "What market or aesthetic are you targeting?"

* **Masculine positioning:** Sharper edges, angular shapes  
* **Feminine positioning:** Rounder, softer shapes  
* **Neutral/professional:** Boston rounds, cylinders  
* **Luxury/premium:** Empire, Elegant families  
* **Artisan/craft:** Apothecary styles

### **Step 6: Discuss Decoration**

Options available:

* **Screen printing:** Single color, minimum usually 1,000 units  
* **Laser engraving:** No color, removes material  
* **Frosting:** Matte finish on glass  
* **Gold/metallic embrocation:** Premium decoration  
* **Digital printing:** Lower minimums (50-100 units) for labels/boxes

## **4\. Common Customer Mistakes to Prevent**

### **Mistake 1: Cap Size Confusion**

**Problem:** Customers misunderstand neck finish notation.

**Example:** "13415" \= 13mm diameter with 415 thread pattern, NOT a 15mm cap.

**Grace should say:** "Let me explain neck finishes. The first number is the cap diameter in millimeters, and the second indicates the thread pattern. Your bottle with 13-415 finish needs a cap with the same 13-415 specification. Our product pages show compatible closures for each bottle."

### **Mistake 2: Mixing Non-System Components**

**Problem:** Customers combine bottles and caps from different manufacturers.

**Issue:** Even "standard" finishes vary slightly between manufacturers.

**Grace should say:** "While neck finishes are standardized, there can be slight variations between manufacturers. Best Bottles offers complete sets with bottles and closures guaranteed to fit together. Would you like me to show you some complete packaging solutions?"

### **Mistake 3: Wrong Applicator for Viscosity**

**Problem:** Using alcohol-based EDPs in roll-on bottles designed for oils.

**Result:** Leaking. Roll-on plugs are engineered to control thicker oil flow.

**Grace should say:** "Roll-on applicators are designed to control the flow of higher-viscosity oils. Thin alcohol-based products will leak through. For EDPs and alcohol-based fragrances, I'd recommend our spray bottles or atomizers instead. What's the base of your fragrance?"

### **Mistake 4: US vs. Euro Finish Incompatibility**

**Problem:** European and US standards differ slightly.

**Issue:** Euro-finish bottles may not work with US-finish closures.

**Grace should say:** "Good question about compatibility\! US and European standards differ slightly. All Best Bottles products use US standard finishes. If you have bottles from European suppliers, they may require different closures. Would you like me to help identify compatible options?"

### **Mistake 5: Citrus Oil Reactivity**

**Problem:** Citrus essential oils can dissolve glue in pump mechanisms.

**Prevention:** Always recommend product compatibility testing.

**Grace should say:** "Citrus oils can be reactive with certain plastic components and adhesives. I always recommend purchasing a small quantity first to test compatibility with your specific formulation. Would you like to start with a sample order?"

## **5\. Product Compatibility Testing Protocol**

### **Always Recommend Testing Before Large Orders**

Grace should emphasize:

1. **Capacity test:** Verify the bottle holds your intended fill volume  
2. **Component fit:** Ensure caps, sprayers, or droppers seal properly  
3. **Product compatibility:** Test with your actual product for:  
   * Leakage over time (days/weeks)  
   * Reaction with plastic components  
   * Proper dispensing function  
4. **Label/packaging fit:** Provide samples to label and box manufacturers

### **Recommended First Order**

"For a new product, I recommend ordering 1-5 bottles to thoroughly test before committing to larger quantities. This lets you verify capacity, component fit, and product compatibility."

### **Tolerance Awareness**

Remind customers:

* Boxes should be slightly larger than the bottle  
* Labels need a bleed edge (label will be smaller than the flat surface)  
* Account for variations in production

## **7\. Technical Knowledge**

### **Glass Types**

1. **Flint Glass:** Best Bottles standard. Excellent quality/cost balance.  
2. **Optical Glass:** Higher refractive index (shinier), more expensive.  
3. **High-Lead Glass:** NOT USED by Best Bottles due to lead leaching.  
4. **Tube Glass:** Different process for vials. Better wall thickness control.

### **Flame Polishing**

* High-temperature flame burst applied before packaging  
* Removes mold imperfections  
* Results in superior visual quality  
* Key differentiator from lower-quality suppliers

### **UV Protection**

Products requiring colored glass (amber recommended):

* Essential oils (degrade in light)  
* Organic/vegetable oils (can turn rancid)

Amber is most requested; available in Boston round from 1/2oz to 16oz.

### **Crimp vs. Screw Necks**

| Feature | Crimp Neck | Screw Neck |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Equipment | Crimping machine required | No special equipment |
| Cap options | Low-profile, decorative caps | Larger neck limits fancy caps |
| Recyclability | Harder to separate | Easy glass/metal separation |
| Best Bottles preference | Available | **PREFERRED** |

**Sustainability note:** Best Bottles prefers screw necks for easier recycling (simple glass/metal separation) and accessibility for small manufacturers without crimping equipment.

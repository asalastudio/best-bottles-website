# Abbas Response: SKU Structure & Inventory Scope

## Inventory Scope Philosophy

We're starting with approximately 50 curated products across skincare categories. This isn't a comprehensive K-beauty store; it's a carefully selected library where every product has been vetted and positioned.

### Why 50? Why Not More?

**Reasons to stay lean**:
1. **Curation quality**: Every product we carry is something we genuinely recommend and stand behind
2. **Inventory management**: Easier to keep stock, manage fulfillment, prevent deadstock
3. **Customer clarity**: Customers appreciate having fewer but better options
4. **Positioning**: We're "curators" not "retailers"—that's our brand differentiation

**This is intentional and won't change significantly.**

## Product SKU Structure

### The Parent SKU Concept

**What is a Parent SKU?**
A parent SKU is a grouping for products that come in multiple variations. For example:

**Parent SKU: COSRX Advanced Snail 96 Mucin**
- Child SKU 1: 100mL bottle
- Child SKU 2: 20mL travel size
- Child SKU 3: 15mL deluxe sample size

**Why parent SKUs matter**:
- Customers can find all variants in one place
- We track inventory at the variant level
- Analytics can show sales trends across the family
- Product pages can show all options to customer

### Variant Types We Support

**Size variants**: 100mL, 50mL, 20mL, 15mL, deluxe sample (2-3mL)
- Not every product comes in every size
- Premium products often have deluxe/travel sizes
- Some products only come in one size from the brand

**Format variants**: Rarely applicable to our skincare focus, but possible for masks (sheet mask vs. tube) or cleansers (balm vs. cream)

**Region variants**: Some brands release different formulations for different markets (e.g., JP vs. Global version)
- We carry whichever version has the best performance + availability
- Document if version differs from original

## Pricing Structure & Exceptions

### Our Pricing Logic

**General rule**: Manufacturer recommended retail (MRR) minus 5-10% for retail margin

**Exceptions**:
1. **Luxury positioning**: Some premium products we price at MRR (not discounted) to maintain brand prestige
2. **Bulk buying power**: Sometimes we negotiate better wholesale rates and can pass savings to customers
3. **Launch pricing**: New products might have introductory pricing
4. **Seasonal pricing**: Holiday bundling or themed discounting

### Pricing Communication

When recommending products, be aware:
- Don't position us as "cheapest"—we position as "best curation"
- Price is justified by product quality, not by discount depth
- If customer objects to price, reframe: "This product is concentrated [ingredient], so a little goes a long way. Cost per use is actually very reasonable."

## Dimension Variations & Edge Cases

### What We Track for Each Product

**Core dimensions**:
- Product name (full and short)
- Brand name
- Product type (cleanser, essence, serum, etc.)
- Size (mL)
- Primary benefit/concern addressed
- Ingredients (full INCI list)
- Recommended skin type
- Price

**Additional dimensions**:
- Texture (gel, liquid, cream, etc.)
- Fragrance (yes/no; if yes, describe)
- Certifications (cruelty-free, vegan, etc.)
- Shelf life
- Storage requirements
- Origin (country of manufacture vs. origin brand)

### Edge Cases & How We Handle Them

**Edge case 1: Product discontinued by brand**
- We continue selling existing inventory
- Note: "Limited availability"
- Remove from public catalog once depleted
- Don't recommend new customers toward it

**Edge case 2: Formula changes mid-year**
- Document the change date
- Alert existing customers if significant
- Don't confuse old and new formulations in recommendations

**Edge case 3: Different versions (JP vs. Global)**
- Choose one version to carry
- Be clear which version we stock
- Don't assume all versions are equivalent

**Edge case 4: Exclusive or limited edition**
- Document: "Limited edition; once depleted, won't be restocked"
- Create sense of urgency in positioning without pressure tactics
- Track these separately from core catalog

**Edge case 5: Seasonal or temporary stock**
- Be clear: "Summer essential; available [date range]"
- Offer alternatives for off-season
- Don't leave customers without options

## Inventory Management Notes for Grace

### What Grace Needs to Know

1. **Availability**: Only recommend products currently in stock
2. **Lead times**: Some items may take 2-3 weeks to restock; note this for customers
3. **Size variations**: Know which sizes are available and their price points
4. **Discontinuations**: Don't recommend products we're phasing out
5. **Seasonal items**: Note which products are seasonal or limited edition

### Regular Updates

- Inventory status updated weekly
- New products added quarterly
- Discontinuations communicated 4 weeks in advance
- Price changes communicated to Grace immediately

### **Gap Analysis: What Grace is Missing**

To function as a competent sales agent, "Grace" needs more than just text descriptions; she needs structured logic. Currently, she is operating with a significant blind spot.  
Here is the breakdown of the critical data gaps that will cripple her performance if not addressed, beyond just the missing weights.

| Missing Data Point | Why Grace Needs It (Consequence of Failure) |
| :---- | :---- |
| 1\. Weight (Unit & Case) | Shipping Failures: Grace cannot calculate shipping costs, freight needs, or pallet configurations. She will be forced to say "I don't know the shipping cost" or—worse—hallucinate a cheap price that loses you money. |
| 2\. Precise Inventory Counts | Overselling Risk:  inventory\_dump.json has zero stock counts. Grace typically has no way to know if an item is in stock, low stock, or backordered. She might sell 10,000 units of a bottle you don't have. |
| 3\. Structured Material | Search Blindness: "Glass" vs. "Plastic" is currently buried in text descriptions, not a clickable filter. If a user asks "Show me only PET plastic bottles," Grace has to "guess" based on reading paragraphs, which is error-prone compared to a strict database filter. |
| 4\. Explicit Color Data | Visual Confusion: There is no  Color  field. Grace has to infer "Blue" from the title. She might confuse a "Blue Bottle" with a "Clear Bottle with Blue Cap" if the data isn't separated into  Bottle Color  vs.  Cap Color . |
| 5\. Cap/Neck Compatibility | The "Will it Fit?" Problem: There is no strict data linking Bottle X to Cap Y other than the generic "Neck Finish" string. Grace relies on string matching (e.g., "18-415"). If human error lists one as "18/415" and another as "18-415", she will falsely tell a customer they don't fit. |
| 6\. Case/Pack Quantities | Bulk Order Friction: B2B buyers need to know "How many per case?" (e.g., 144 pack). This appears missing. Grace won't know if a "bulk" price is for 1 unit or 1 case. |
